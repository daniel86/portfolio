Digital Twins & Knowledge-based Robotics for Retail

In the REFILLS project and related research, we explored how digital twins and knowledge-based AI can transform retail robotics and store operations. I developed a rich semantic digital twin of supermarkets: a knowledge graph and ontology that formally models store layouts, shelves, products, and their relations. This model enabled autonomous robots to perform semantic mapping — scanning a store and automatically building a digital twin that can support navigation, perception, and decision-making.

Beyond mapping, we demonstrated a wide range of Industry 4.0 applications. Using Unreal Engine, we built immersive VR experiences where managers could explore new or remote stores virtually without travel. For customers, we developed mobile applications where product knowledge was linked to the semantic layer, offering enhanced shopping assistance and personalized product information. In all cases, the semantic twin was the backbone that allowed agents (human or robotic) to reason and make informed decisions.

A key aspect of the work was integration with existing enterprise systems. We augmented ERP data streams (SAP) with the semantic knowledge graph, enriching operational information with context for tasks such as shelf restocking, product placement optimization, and logistics planning. These integrations aligned the research with the Industry 4.0 vision of connected, intelligent production and retail environments.

To foster adoption, I implemented interactive WebGL visualizations integrated with live robot telemetry via Robot Web Tools. These demonstrators — shown at major industrial fairs such as CEBIT and Hannover Messe — helped bridge the gap between research and practice. This transfer ultimately contributed to the founding of Ubica Robotics, a spin-off now operating commercial robot fleets and awarded the euRobotics Technology Transfer Award 2022.

Project highlights

  • Designed and implemented a semantic digital twin architecture with ontologies and knowledge graphs for retail stores.
  • Developed autonomous semantic mapping algorithms for robots to generate digital twins of real supermarkets.
  • Built VR-based store exploration prototypes in Unreal Engine and mobile shopping assistant apps using the semantic knowledge base.
  • Integrated ERP data (SAP) with semantic layers to enable decision support for replenishment, placement, and logistics.
  • Implemented WebGL-based visualizations with ROS integration for interactive demos at CEBIT & Hannover Messe.
  • Facilitated industry transfer activities, contributing to the foundation and success of Ubica Robotics.

Role: Semantic modeling lead & visualization developer — responsible for ontology design, ERP integration, and VR/visualization development.
