Terrain Rendering System

The terrain pipeline in regen targets large outdoor environments with stable frame times and high visual variety. It combines a quad-tree LOD mesh with SIMD-accelerated CPU culling and GPU compute culling, and uses adaptive skirt meshes to eliminate cracks at LOD seams and under heightmap displacement. Materials are blended by physically meaningful signals—height, slope, concavity, and optional masks—producing context-aware transitions between sand, grass, rock, and dirt.

The example scene in the screenshots is a canyon with a four-material setup (sand/grass/rock/dirt), biome-aware vegetation (grass sprites and trees), and a water pass running through the valley (with fish simulated by the boids system). Screenshots include a foggy variant, pure albedo to show material blending, the generated weight maps, wireframe LOD (with skirts), and large-scale grass rendering.

Shading is driven by a set of GLSL utilities: global ground UVs for atlas lookups, tri-planar mapping for local detail, and a compact material combiner that selects and normalizes the top-N contributing materials per fragment to keep the cost predictable. A lightweight biome model uses height, slope, northness, temperature/moisture heuristics, rockiness, and concavity (via a 4-neighbor Laplacian) to steer distribution of assets and surface detail. Optional noise perturbations add micro-variation to both color and normals without sacrificing stability.

Project highlights

  • Quad-tree LOD with skirts — crack-free tile boundaries via adaptive skirt meshes; wireframe captures show multiple LODs and skirt geometry along patch edges.
  • CPU + GPU culling — SIMD-optimized frustum checks on CPU and compute-shader culling on GPU; integrates with regen’s draw-indirect workflow.
  • Signal-driven material blending — weights derived from height/slope/concavity and optional masks; branch-light, top-N selection keeps shading cost bounded.
  • Tri-planar material mapping — consistent high-frequency detail without UV seams; normal composition with TBN built from world-space normals.
  • Biome synthesis — temperature, moisture, rockiness, and northness steer materials and asset placement (grass/trees), producing plausible ecotones.
  • Large-scale grass — GPU sprite grass with wind animation; density modulated by biome weights and noise for natural clumping.
  • Water integration — separate water pass with reflection/refraction support; meshes coexist cleanly with terrain skirts.
  • Tight engine integration — works with regen’s LOD, culling, impostors, and boids; materials and biomes are configured through the engine’s staging system.

Role: end-to-end design and implementation (quad-tree LOD, skirt meshes, CPU/GPU culling paths, material/biome system, grass renderer, and the supporting GLSL utilities) and integration into regen’s resource/staging and draw pipeline.
