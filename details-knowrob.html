KnowRob 2.0 — Knowledge Processing for Cognition-enabled Robotic Agents

KnowRob 2.0 is a second-generation knowledge processing framework that combines logic programming, formal ontologies, and knowledge graphs to enable robots to understand, explain, and verify their actions. It provides a shared platform for reasoning across perception, planning, and execution, forming the semantic backbone of the EASE Collaborative Research Center and other major projects.

At its core, KnowRob integrates symbolic and sub-symbolic knowledge, unifies heterogeneous data sources, and provides a hybrid query-answering layer that supports temporal reasoning, spatial inference, and task introspection. The system’s design enables scalable knowledge integration, linking facts from Prolog knowledge bases, RDF triple stores, and MongoDB databases into a coherent knowledge graph.

A key element of the project was the development of the SOMA ontology — a rich semantic model that formalizes objects, activities, roles, dispositions, and affordances. This ontology allows robots to reason about what actions are possible in a given context, and to explain successes or failures in terms of object properties and causal dependencies.

More recently, KnowRob has been extended to work with semantic digital twins — representations of real or simulated environments as queryable knowledge graphs. This includes translating Universal Scene Description (USD) scene graphs into formal knowledge representations, enabling semantic labeling of game/VR environments and robotic simulations with the same fidelity used in physical robot experiments.

Project highlights

  • Hybrid KRR architecture: combination of logic programming, ontology reasoning, and data-driven methods for temporal, spatial, and causal reasoning.
  • MongoLog: a Prolog-like query language running directly over MongoDB for scalable knowledge storage and inference.
  • SOMA ontology: formalization of objects, activities, dispositions, and affordances to support decision making and explanation.
  • Semantic digital twins: knowledge-graph-based representations linking perception, execution traces, and simulation environments.
  • Semantic USD: proposed method for translating USD scene graphs into knowledge graphs for semantic labeling of VR/game environments.
  • Cross-project adoption: KnowRob is widely used within EASE and beyond, with more than 1000 citations between 2017–2024 (2000+ overall).

Role: I was the lead developer of KnowRob 2.0, designed and implemented the logic-based interface layer and major subsystems, co-designed the overall architecture, and was corresponding author of the highly cited ICRA paper that introduced the system.
